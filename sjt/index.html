History

San Juan, La Union, holds a storied past rooted in indigenous heritage and shaped by colonial influences. In ancient times, indigenous tribes inhabited these lands, leaving behind cultural imprints that endure today. Spanish colonization marked a significant chapter in San Juan's history, transforming it into a pueblo centered around agriculture while imprinting Spanish traditions on the region.

In recent decades, San Juan embraced a new identity as the surfing capital of the north. Its pristine beaches and consistent waves attracted surfers globally, leading to a tourism surge that injected vitality into the town while maintaining its relaxed ambiance.

Among the contemporary developments, San Juan's heritage remains tangible. The municipality hall, an aged structure, stands as a symbol of the town's historical legacy.

Today, as travelers stroll its vibrant streets and soak in its golden beaches, they witness the harmonious coexistence of the town's past and present. San Juan, La Union, offers a captivating blend of ancient history and modern allure, inviting visitors to explore its diverse heritage and vibrant contemporary lifestyle.
